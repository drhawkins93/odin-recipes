Roast Chicken

Description

This dish is one of my favorites. It will blow away anyone who comes over for dinner, and it's deceptively simple to make! Pair it with some homemade mashed potatoes and roasted broccoli and your date will be thoroughly impressed.

If you don't already have a roasting pan, I highly recommend picking one up. You'll also need to plan ahead, since this dish is best prepared starting a day in advance.

Ingredients

  • One three- to six- pound raw chicken
  • Unsalted butter
  • Rosemary
  • Thyme
  • Sage
  • Garlic
  • Celery
  • Kosher salt
  • Black pepper

Steps

  1. First, drain the chicken as much as possible. Coat the outside (top and bottom) in kosher salt, and leave uncovered in the fridge for at least twenty-four hours.
  2. When you are ready to start preparing the chicken, preheat the oven to 350.
  3. After the chicken has dry-brined overnight, its skin should be nice and dessicated. Remove the chicken from the fridge and let it sit out so it can get closer to room temperature.
  4. Soften the butter in the microwave. Finely mince the rosemary, thyme, and sage, and mix it into the butter.
  5. Rub the whole chicken down with the herb butter. Be sure to get as much as you can under the skin as well!
  6. Stuff the chicken with celery stalks and garlic cloves.
  7. Bake the chicken for roughly two hours. You'll know when it's done when its internal temperature has reached 165 degrees.
  8. After removing the chicken from the oven, allow it to rest for at least twenty minutes before carving it.
